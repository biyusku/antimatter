## CERN Production Chain

- **AD (Antiproton Decelerator):** 10¹³ protons → iridium target → 5 × 10⁷ antiprotons/minute at 26 GeV/c
- **ELENA ring (30m hexagonal, operational ~2021):** decelerates AD output from 5.3 MeV → 0.1 MeV
- **Old degrader foil method:** lost 99.9% of antiprotons; ELENA bypasses this with electromagnetic deceleration → 10–100× more usable antiprotons

## ALPHA Experiment — Key Numbers

- **First trapping:** November 2010 — 38 atoms, 0.17 seconds
- **Confinement record (published):** June 2011 — 1,000 seconds (16.7 min); up to 3 atoms simultaneously
- **Routine production:** tens to ~100 antihydrogen atoms trapped per experimental cycle
- **CPT precision:** 200 parts per trillion (1S–2S spectroscopy)
- **Laser cooling:** first achieved 2021
- **ALPHA-g (2023):** confirmed antihydrogen falls DOWN; ~20% precision; _Nature_ 621, 716–722, Sept 27 2023

> ⚠️ "100+ hours confinement" claim in transcript NOT verified — published record is 1,000 s. May conflate charged-particle storage with neutral antihydrogen trapping.

## Penning Trap Basics

- **Axial confinement:** electric quadrupole (ring + hyperboloidal endcaps; voltage V₀)
- **Radial confinement:** axial magnetic field B (1–7 Tesla superconducting)
- **Three modes:** axial (ω_z), modified cyclotron (ω₊), magnetron (ω₋); sum ω₊ + ω₋ = ω_c
- **Operating temp:** ~4 K (liquid helium); UHV 10⁻¹¹–10⁻¹² mbar
- **Why neutral antihydrogen is harder:** magnetic gradient trap well depth only ~0.5 K vs. ~11,600 K for charged-particle Penning trap — 20,000× shallower

## Alternative Production

- **Laser-driven (LLNL 2008):** petawatt laser + gold foil → positrons at record density; not yet trappable
- **CERN/Oxford (2023):** 440 GeV beam + C-Ta target → 1.5 × 10¹³ e⁺e⁻ pairs in one shot (record)
- **Lightning (TGFs):** ~10¹¹–10¹² positrons per event (FERMI telescope detected)
- **Van Allen belts:** ~10 μg total antiprotons (PAMELA, 2011); energy 60–750 MeV; South Atlantic Anomaly

## Natural Sources Summary

- **Cosmic rays:** continuous antiproton/positron rain; PAMELA satellite measured fluxes
- **β⁺ decay:** F-18, C-11 for PET scanners (~40M scans/year globally) — most widespread antimatter application
- **Van Allen belts:** 10 μg antiprotons ≈ CERN's entire 20-year output; inaccessible for practical use

## Fermilab (Historical)

- Tevatron antiproton stacking: ~2–6 × 10¹⁰ antiprotons/hour at peak
- Tevatron shutdown: **September 30, 2011**
- No Western Hemisphere facility currently produces research-scale antiprotons

## BASE-STEP Portable Trap

- Persistent-current superconducting magnets (no external power after charging)
- First antiprotons moved outside AD hall
- ~1 hour storage demonstrated
- Foundation for remote-lab and space antimatter experiments
